MEDITATION

“Meditation” or “Dhyana” means to go beyond the limitations of the physical body and the mind. Only when you transcend the limited perspective of the body and the mind do you have a complete dimension of life within you. When you are identified as the body, your whole life is only about survival.

Why Meditation?

There are only Two types of suffering in this world: physical and mental. Once this distance becomes a constant factor in your experience, you have reached the end of suffering. With elimination of the fear of suffering, you can walk life full stride, unafraid to explore all that life has to offer. Your ability to use this immensely sophisticated body-mind phenomenon can be raised to completely new dimension of experience. Meditation is a journey toward a reality in which you experience the ultimate nature of existence as borderless unity.

How to do it?

Shiva gave 112 ways of achieving tranquilty of mind. But the most common one is: Breath Awareness .

Step 1:

  • Sit with straight back
  • Box Breath to normalise Breathing pattern, i.e inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds and exhale for 4 seconds
  • When breath normalises, focus on breath and breath normally, dont force breath.
  • Your mind will wander many times, it will tell you stories, will show you glimpses of past.
  • Be gentle towards yourself when you find yourself wandering in thoughts bring you attention of breath with ease.
  • After doing this regularly for about 21 days, you will find yourself in bliss internally as you should be, you will have clarity of mind and your working efficiency will immense.
